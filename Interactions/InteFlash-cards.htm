Though often used interchangeably, the words below have distinct meanings and etymologies. Flip each card to learn more about each term.

Black ↻

The term "Black" is considered more universal, as it points to a global community and includes members of the African diaspora.

African American ↻

Refers specifically to American-born individuals of African descent, emphasizing both American-ness and African heritage.

People of Color ↻

An inclusive term referring to all non-white racial and ethnic groups, emphasizing shared experiences of systemic racism.

The Black community in the United States initially adopted the term "African American" as a way of asserting both their American-ness and their African heritage.

But the default term "Black" is considered more universal, as it points to a global community and includes members of the African diaspora.
